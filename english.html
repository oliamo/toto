The oil

From the olive grove of Grandpa Totó, we bring the sun of Sicily directly to your table – with the finest extra virgin olive oil from the 2024 harvest. In San Cipirello, Grandpa Totó planted a carefully selected mix of Nocellara del Belice (30%), Cerasuola (30%), Biancolilla (30%), and Calamignana (10%) olive trees. He lovingly cared for them and passed them on to the next generations, who have continued to care for the olive grove ever since. The trees are left in their natural state, except for the necessary pruning, and are neither fertilized nor sprayed or treated in any other way. The olive tree is perfectly suited for dry farming and has developed biological mechanisms to cope with water scarcity and thrive even under challenging climatic conditions. However, it does require a minimal amount of water, which in Grandpa Totó's olive grove comes solely from the sky. For this reason – and especially given the changing climate – the quantity of olives, and thus the oil production, may vary from year to year. If you would like to have Oliamo personally on your table, you can sponsor an olive tree. Depending on the harvest, we will send you your share of olive oil. If you're interested, simply send us a message! We'll be happy to provide you with more information. Thanks to our minimalist care, the olives grow in complete freedom and untouched, which is reflected in the purity and unique flavor of our olive oil. In collaboration with the Frantolio from San Giuseppe Jato, we have developed a wonderfully robust yet balanced olive oil. Through direct, local processing and cold pressing of the olives at 25°C, we have produced a high-quality extra virgin olive oil. You can learn more about our production process here.

Why oliamo?

The unique blend of olives from Grandpa Totó's grove makes our extra virgin olive oil a true delight. Throughout the production process, we collaborate with many people and are committed to creating an ecologically valuable and sustainably produced olive oil for the future. oliamo stands exactly for this goal. The name of our olive oil is made up of parts of the word "Oliva" (Oli) and the verb form "iamo" (we do). It also contains the word "amo" (love), which symbolizes the bond between Elena and Justus. In San Cipirello, the hometown of Grandpa Totó (Elena's grandfather), people from different cultures have lived and worked together for centuries. The oldest known traces of human settlements date back to the 7th century. Since then, various cultures have settled in Sicily and enriched it culturally. One product of this cultural exchange is the cultivation of olives – and thus, our olive oil. We are excited to involve you in this exchange through oliamo.

The production process

The most important step in the production of our olive oil is the harvest – a strenuous, sweat-inducing, and labor-intensive process. First, we shake the branches so that the slightly loosened olives fall onto the nets spread out beneath the trees. Then, we comb the trees with an electric rake to strip off the olives that are still firmly attached. The olives that fall onto the nets are collected onto a trailer and taken to the press within the next 48 hours. After weighing, the leaves are removed, and the olives are washed and ground. The resulting olive paste is mixed and stirred for about 30 minutes at 25°C. During this process, the oil begins to separate from the paste but is then further extracted in a press. Finally, the oil is purified in a centrifuge to remove any remaining water. The result is the wonderfully delicious Oliamo olive oil from Grandpa Totó.
