The oil

From Grandpa Totó's olive grove, we bring the sun of Sicily directly to your table - with the best extra virgin olive oil from the 2024 harvest. In San Cipirello, Grandpa Totó planted an exquisite blend of Nocellara del Belice (30 %), Cerasuola (30 %), Biancolilla (30 %) and Calamignana (10 %) olive trees. He tended them lovingly and passed them on to the next generations, who have been looking after the olive grove ever since. Apart from the necessary pruning, the trees are left in their natural state and are not fertilised, sprayed or treated in any other way. The olive tree is perfectly suited to dry cultivation and has developed biological mechanisms to cope with a lack of water and thrive even under difficult climatic conditions. Nevertheless, it only needs a minimum of water, which in the olive groves of Opa Totó comes exclusively from the sky. For this reason - and especially in view of the changing climate - the quantity of olives and therefore also the oil production can fluctuate from year to year. The olives grow in the olive grove of Opa Totó in complete freedom and untouched nature, which is reflected in the purity and unique flavour of our olive oil. In collaboration with Frantolio from San Giuseppe Jato and a farmer from the region, we have developed a wonderfully strong and balanced olive oil. By processing the olives directly, locally and cold pressing them at 25°C - always within 48 hours of harvest - we have produced a high-quality, extra virgin olive oil. Why oliamo?

Grandpa Totó's olive trees in San Cipirello have been thriving under the Sicilian sun for many years, producing fewer olives every year. Since Grandpa Totó no longer looks after the trees and his land himself, but has left the land and the plants to his descendants, the care of the trees has diminished more and more, and with it the yield. In order to preserve Sicily's cultural landscape and keep Grandpa Totó's olive trees alive for many years to come, we have decided to take more care of the olive grove again and look forward to letting you taste the first fruits of this labour with oliamo 2024. The unique blend of olives from Grandpa Totó's grove makes our oliamo a true delight. During production, we work closely with local partners to create an ecologically valuable and sustainably produced olive oil. That's exactly what oliamo stands for. The name of our olive oil is made up of parts of the word Oliva (oli) and the verb form iamo (we make). In addition, oliamo contains the word amo (love), which connects Elena and Justus. In San Cipirello, the hometown of Grandpa Totó (Elena's grandfather), people from different cultures have lived and worked together for centuries. The oldest known traces of human settlement date back to the 7th century. Since then, various cultures have populated Sicily and enriched it culturally. A product of this cultural exchange is the cultivation of olives - and so is oliamo. With oliamo we would like to let you take part in this tradition – by enjoying our olive oil. The production process

The most important step on the way to our olive oil is harvesting the olives - a strenuous, sweaty and laborious process. Before we shake the olives from the trees, we have to cover the ground with nets on which we collect the olives. Only then do we shake the trees and branches so that the already somewhat loose olives fall onto the nets. We then comb the trees with an electric rake to remove the olives that are still stuck. The olives lying on the nets are collected on a trailer and taken for pressing within the next 48 hours. After the leaves are removed, the olives are weighed and then washed and immediately ground. The resulting olive pulp is mixed and stirred for about 30 minutes at 25 °C. The oil separates from the pulp, but is still extracted in a press. The oil is then cleaned of water in a centrifuge. The result is Grandpa Totó's wonderfully delicious oliamo olive oil.
